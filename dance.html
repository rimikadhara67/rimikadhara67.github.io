My Dance Journey

I started dancing at the young age of 4. While I immersed myself into Bollywood songs and dance style at home, I started studying the Indian classical dance, Kathak, under the guidance of Guru Smt. Bhavana Lele in Thane, Mumbai, India. The word 'Katha' means story and thus, the dance style of Kathak focuses on the art of storytelling while keeping gestures, elegance, footwork, and flow of movements at the forefront of its technique. I have been practicing this technique for about 15 years now through several performances depicting Indian mythology such as tales from Ramayana and Mahabharata. I also have attended several workshops under the late Guru Pt. Birju Maharaj and his disciple, Guru Shaswati Sen, who are both exceptionally distinguished maestros of the art of Kathak. Upon moving to the United States at the age of 14, I continued learning under Guru Rita Mustaphi as an Intern at Katha Dance Theatre (KDT). Through KDT, I have been able to accentuate several societal issues through my performances such as Women's rights, Human Rights, Equality, etc.

Other than Kathak, I have been extremely involved in dance styles such as Bollywood, contemporary, semi-classical, hip-hop, and bhangra. Currently, I am the Manager for Minnesota Junoon – Minnesota's Premier Bollywood Fusion Competitive Dance Team. In previous years, we have organized 8-10 minutes long sets integrating several different dance styles while inculcating entertaining themes such as Taco Bell (2021-22). We spend approximately 15 hours every week on these sets leading up to our competitions which are usually in different states across the country such as Philadelphia (PA), Atlanta (GA), Austin (TX), and San Diego (CA).

Overall, my passion for dance transcends through several outlets whether it be small or big. I am currently working on some projects of my own and can't wait to showcase them on here. I feel extremely grateful to have learnt such a rewarding art form and I can't wait for my future endeavors in this field!
